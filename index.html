Rules of Engagement

Make sure to read and comply with the regulations of the CTF arena.

Fair Play

No sharing of flags, writeups, or solutions during the event. Team cooperation is allowed only within registered team members. Collaboration between different teams is strictly forbidden and will lead to disqualification.

Prohibited Actions

Attacking the scoring infrastructure, hosting platforms, or other contestants is prohibited. Do not use automated scanners (like SQLMap, Nikto, or Nessus) against challenges unless explicitly permitted in the challenge description.

Flag Submission

Flags are formatted as CS2026{s0me_vAlue_h3re} unless otherwise specified. Submitting false flags repeatedly in rapid succession (brute-forcing) may trigger automated IP bans on the challenge platform.

Support & Hints

For questions or platform issues, open a ticket on Discord. Hints are available for some challenges but might carry a point penalty. Make sure to review the hints page carefully before revealing.

FAQ

Who can participate in this CTF?

This event is open to students, security practitioners, hobbyists, and anyone interested in cyber security. Anyone from any geographical location can register and compete.

What is the maximum team size?

Teams can consist of 1 to 4 individuals. You can register as a solo player or join an existing team via your user dashboard.

Are the challenge files safe to download?

Yes, all files hosted on our platforms are safe. However, when working with binary exploitation or malware analysis challenges, we highly recommend executing and analyzing the files inside a safe, isolated Virtual Machine (VM) or sandbox environment.
